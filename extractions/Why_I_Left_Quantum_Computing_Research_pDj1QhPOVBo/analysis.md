## Conclusions

**Main Arguments:**
1. **Algorithm Scarcity**: The fundamental issue isn't hardware limitations but the severe lack of practical quantum algorithms
2. **Research Misallocation**: Too much focus on hardware and error correction, insufficient attention to algorithm development  
3. **Technical Limitations**: The measurement problem creates inherent challenges in extracting useful information from quantum computations
4. **Uncertain Future Utility**: Even with perfect quantum hardware, current applications would be limited

**Current Assessment (2025):**
- Hardware progress has been impressive and on track
- Algorithm development remains stagnant with minimal breakthroughs
- The speaker's 2020 concerns appear largely validated
- One new theoretical algorithm discovered in 2023, though not practically implementable

**Overall Sentiment:**
Despite the criticisms, the speaker remains cautiously optimistic about quantum computing's future but emphasizes the critical need for more algorithm research to justify the massive hardware investments.
